Eman Gouda is an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Computer Science at Alexandria University. She received her PhD in Computer Vision from the Mohamed bin Zayed University of Artificial Intelligence (MBZUAI). Her research interests lie in machine learning and computer vision, with a particular focus on multi-modal adaptation and unsupervised learning. In addition to her research, she contributes to the academic community through peer review and service roles for leading conferences such as CVPR, ECCV, and NeurIPS.

Research Interests

Generally interested in machine learning and computer vision, with a goal of building general-purpose intelligence that can see, reason, and act reliably in the unpredictable world. My current research focuses on vision-language models, multi-modal adaptation, and unsupervised learning frameworks, with a particular interest in enhancing the scalability and accuracy of foundational models.
